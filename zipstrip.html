ZIP Strip
— Brand & Packaging

Logo, brand guidelines, and a full retail and trade packaging system for a new LED strip line — built from nothing.

Overview

Creating a brand from a blank canvas

ZIP Strip is Harled’s dedicated LED COB strip lighting line — a product range that needed its own identity, voice, and physical presence on shelf. When I took it on there was nothing: no logo, no brand language, no packaging system, no guidelines. Just a product name and a substantial range of kits waiting to be given a face.

The range is complex: single colour kits in 5M, 10M, and 15M lengths across 2700K/3K/4K/6K, cut-free variants, IP67 waterproof kits, RGB, CCT, RGBCCT, and IC addressable options — each with its own driver, remote, and connector configuration. Every SKU needed outer packaging, a product insert, installation instructions, and a cutter guide.

My role as sole designer was to build the entire brand from scratch — logo and wordmark, brand guidelines, outer retail and trade packaging, and the full product insert system including installation guides and cutter templates for every variant.

Challenge vs. Response
Challenge

No brand existed at all — blank canvas. The name ZIP Strip was set, but everything else needed to be created from zero.

Response

Built the brand identity from the ground up: defining the visual language, creating the wordmark, establishing a colour system and typographic hierarchy that felt current, technical, and distinct.

Challenge

The packaging had to work in two very different contexts — retail shelves where it competes visually with established brands, and trade environments where spec clarity matters most.

Response

Designed a dual-purpose packaging system: strong visual identity and brand presence for retail, with a clear spec hierarchy and variant differentiation system for trade.

Challenge

The ZIP Strip range spans multiple SKUs — different lengths, CCTs, IP ratings — all needing to feel like one coherent brand while remaining clearly distinguishable from each other.

Response

Designed a modular packaging architecture with a consistent colour and label coding system for CCT, IP rating, and length variants — making the range scannable and systematic without losing visual cohesion.

A brand isn’t a logo. It’s every touchpoint — the box on the shelf, the label on the reel, the language on the carton, and the guidelines that make sure it stays consistent long after the designer leaves the room.

— ZIP Strip, 2025
Process

From blank canvas to shelf-ready brand

01
Brand strategy + naming the language
Before any design, defined what ZIP Strip should feel like: technical but accessible, premium without being cold, confident in a market full of generic LED packaging.
02
Logo + wordmark
Designed the ZIP Strip wordmark — built to work across packaging, labels, catalogue pages, and digital assets. A mark that felt energetic and precise, referencing the zip-cut functionality without being literal.
03
Brand guidelines
Documented the full brand system: logo usage rules, colour palette, typography, tone of voice, and application examples across packaging, digital, and print.
04
Retail packaging design
Designed the retail box and carton system across the full ZIP Strip kit range — single colour, cut-free, IP67, RGB, CCT, RGBCCT, and IC addressable variants. Each box carries the brand identity, a clear CCT and IP rating coding system, key spec callouts, and the kit contents.
05
Trade packaging + product inserts
Adapted the system for trade formats — bulk reel cartons, outer packaging, and label designs prioritising spec legibility. Also designed the full insert system: installation instructions, cutting interval guides, and kit contents layouts for every SKU.
