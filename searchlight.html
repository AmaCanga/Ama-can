Searchlight
— Trade Catalogue

Rescuing a 1,300-SKU decorative lighting catalogue from broken files, inconsistent photography, and no clear visual direction.

Overview

Inheriting a mess and delivering a catalogue worth printing

Searchlight is a UK decorative lighting brand with a broad trade catalogue — 1,300 SKUs covering pendants, wall lights, floor lamps, outdoor lanterns, and table lamps. When I came on board to lead the catalogue redesign, the existing assets were in poor shape: InDesign files full of broken links, photography that ranged wildly in style and quality, missing or low-res images across a significant portion of the range, and no coherent visual language.

My role was to lead a small team through a full catalogue rebuild — establishing a new visual direction, resolving the asset problems, reshooting where needed, and delivering a single-volume trade catalogue that felt aspirational and consistent across every page.

Challenge vs. Response
Challenge

Photography was inconsistent throughout — some products shot lifestyle, others on plain white, others missing or too low-res to use.

Response

Set a clear lifestyle-led visual direction: aspirational room-set photography showing products in context. Managed reshoots for missing and substandard imagery.

Challenge

1,300 SKUs across a full decorative range needed a layout system that could flex across product types without losing coherence.

Response

Designed a flexible grid and template system that balanced lifestyle imagery with spec information — letting hero products breathe while keeping supporting SKUs clear and scannable.

Challenge

Leading a small team through a high-volume, deadline-driven project with no established workflow and messy inherited foundations.

Response

Set up a production workflow, split responsibilities by section, and ran regular reviews to keep quality consistent. Acted as both creative lead and production manager throughout.

1,300 products. One visual direction. The job was archaeology, organisation, and art direction all running at the same time.

— Searchlight, 2023
Process

How the catalogue came together

01
Asset audit + file rescue
Opened every inherited InDesign file, catalogued all broken links, and mapped the full state of the asset library. Built a new naming and filing system before anything else.
02
Visual direction + photography brief
Defined a lifestyle-led visual approach: aspirational room-sets, warm residential settings, products shown in the kind of spaces the target buyer aspires to. Briefed and managed reshoots for missing and inconsistent imagery.
03
Template + layout system
Designed a flexible catalogue grid that worked across the full product range. The layout system gave the team a clear production framework while leaving room for creative art direction on feature spreads.
04
Team production + QC
Split the catalogue into sections and managed the team through production. Set up a review process to maintain quality across all pages.
05
Sales materials + social assets
Extended the catalogue visual language into supporting sales materials and digital/social assets — giving the brand a consistent presence across trade print and digital channels.
